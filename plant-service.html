Planting Services


 

Indoor Plants 

Plants have many benefits, including helping with the air quality in your office space. Plants at work can help improve your employees’ stress levels by creating a well oxygenated, calming environment. There are many different ways to add plants at work, they can be in free-standing indoor planters, desktop planters, and even cabinet top planters. Bringing plants indoors can also have more practical uses; they can be used  to section off  walkways and other areas within the office without having to build new partitions or walls. If you have a waiting area, plants are a great way to obscure the view from the waiting area to the main office space. With PlantCare’s hassle-free installation and maintenance service you are guaranteed a stunning plant display for years to come.

Indoor Trees 

Looking for something a little more striking? There are certain types of trees that thrive indoors but you do need to consider how much space you have and how much natural light is available. Trees can be planted in large planters, or even installed into the floor. PlantCare are tree installation and maintenance specialists and can advise on suitable tree types and locations.

Wall Planting 

Indoor wall planting uses the vertical space in your workplace to stunning effect. A wall or divider filled with green plants can add striking colour and focus to an otherwise neutral office. Planted walls allow greater plant density in a smaller floor area for improved air quality. You can even incorporate a moss panel that allows your branding to stand out in a beautiful green backdrop.

What are the benefits of plants? 

Have you ever experienced a sense of calm and tranquility when you are in a garden or park? This is because plants have a positive effect on your health and wellbeing. Most people have an innate connection with the natural world, and plants are the ideal way to stay connected. Rich oxygenation of the air around you can have positive effects on you and your team's mental health, but plants at work can also support physical health. Dry air, for example can cause sore throats, especially in customer care operations. Plants increase humidity which can help to reduce sore throats, coughing, and headaches. Plants at work can even make team members more productive and engaged helping with staff retention and minimising downtime and sickness.
